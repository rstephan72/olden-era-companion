    Neutral Creatures — the map's own army¶

    How neutrals work (verified)¶

    Faction units come in 7 tiers with dual upgrades; neutral creatures break both rules: they span 8 tiers, and cannot be upgraded — one version each. They don't belong to any town. Four documented ways to get them into your army:

    1. Specialized hiring buildings on the adventure map.
    2. Guard rewards — some defeated object-guards join you.
    3. The Chimerologist's Hut — rerolls native troops with a chance of neutral results (note: T8s don't drop from it).
    4. Summoning spells — temporary battle stacks (Starchild, Primal Remnant, and the Avatar are all technically neutral creatures; see their entries in the Nightshade, Primal, and Neutral Magic chapters).

    Tier 8 is the headline: an elite band above every faction's ceiling, guarding the map's richest treasures. The documented example is the Lich Dragon, whose Bone Armageddon ability deals Pure Damage to all other creatures equal to its unit count — a 333-strong guard hits everything on the field for 333 unmodifiable damage. T8 fights are the game's raid bosses: bring answers, not armies.

    The verified roster (21 creatures)¶

    Grouped by the role their identity and sourcing indicate; per-unit stat rows are the queued fetch noted in the audit.

    Fodder & early-map guards: Pixie · Gnat · Peasant · Ghoul · Halfling — the week-1 XP diet. (Peasant is the series' traditional floor; Halfling its traditional ranged nuisance.)

    Mid-map muscle: Animated Armor (Construct — morale/luck locked at 0, immune to those swings, and doesn't feed Necromancy) · Groll · Kittenhorn · Giant Toad · Unicorn.

    Summon-only stacks: Starchild (Nightshade's Summon Starchild) · Primal Remnant (Primal's summon) · Avatar (the hero-scaled champion — the one neutral documented with full formulas, in the Neutral Magic chapter).

    Elite guards & prizes: Faerie Dragon · Couatl · Dragonslayer · Worldwatcher · Sentinel of Glory.

    The dragon tier: Obsidian Dragon · Red Dragon · Lich Dragon (T8) — the treasure-guard class, with the Lich Dragon's Bone Armageddon as the documented apex threat.

    Fighting neutrals well (original analysis)¶

    • Read the type line before the stat line. The combat appendix's type table decides these fights: Constructs ignore your morale debuffs and feed Necromancy nothing; Undead neutrals (Ghoul, Lich Dragon) can't be Slept; Living/Magic Creature guards are Necromancy's richest harvest.
    • Percentage damage beats dragon guards. Guillotine (Arcane, 7 mana, % of max HP, stacking recasts) exists for exactly the high-HP single-stack guards this chapter describes — often cheaper than the army losses of fighting fair.
    • Bone Armageddon math: unmodifiable damage to everything means the counter isn't armor, it's stack count and body doubles — summons eat the same 333 as your real units, for free. A Starchild/Remnant screen turns the T8's signature move into wasted violence. (Reality Distortion's damage-deferral is the expert-tier alternative.)
    • Read Minds / Groundsight first. The intel spells (Nightshade T2, Primal T1) exist for neutral-fight selection; against 8 tiers of guards, knowing exact counts is worth more than any single combat spell.
    • Chimerologist gambling pairs with Primordial Chaos logic: reroll what you can't fight into what you can — but variance cuts both ways; don't reroll solved fights.
