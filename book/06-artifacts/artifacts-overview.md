# Artifacts — the 14-slot game

## How the system works (verified)

- Heroes carry **14 equipment slots** (head, neck, torso, hands, main
  hand, waist, legs, feet, rings, and multiple misc-item slots — misc
  allows four at once). Bonuses apply only **while equipped**.
- **Four rarity categories**; higher rarity = rarer and stronger.
  **Relics** sit at the top with unique effects — their signature
  pattern is *granting every unlearned subskill of specific skill
  trees* (bypassing the pick-one-of-three system entirely).
- Artifacts can be **upgraded with alchemical dust**, improving their
  main bonus.
- **Sets:** each artifact may belong to one. Minimal sets (2 pieces)
  add a bonus when both are worn; expanded sets (up to 8 pieces)
  unlock staged bonuses — typically at 3, 5, and 8 pieces.
- Sources: found on the map, won from guards, or bought from an
  artifact merchant built in your city.

## Why sets matter (verified design intent)

Past hero level ~20, level-ups slow to a crawl — sets are the
documented late-game growth axis, with full-set bonuses described as
playstyle-transforming rather than incremental. Two verified
flagships:

- **Knight's Honor** (Plate Armor, Lance, Misericorde, Armet, Drum of
  War): +5% morale-trigger chance per point — a core piece of the
  20%-per-morale-point stack documented in the combat appendix.
- **Power of the Dragon Father** (8 pieces): full-set grants **magic
  immunity** — Armageddon-build enablement for factions *without*
  Black Dragons or Abyssal Envoys. Partial bonuses at 3/5 are
  modest; this set is all-or-nothing.
- **Tabar Signet-Ring + Ukh Signet** (2-piece): Luck + Morale rings
  whose pairing adds a strong experience bonus — the cheapest set
  payoff in the game.

## The verified standouts, by slot

**Head:** Spellbinder's Hat (adds *all Tier-5 spells* to the
spellbook — an Expert-school shortcut in an item) · Fancy Mask (+2→3
Focus Points at battle start — Murmuring in a hat) · Two-faced Mask
(kill with Heroic Strike → use it again that round).

**Main hand:** Shroomwood Bow (removes the ranged penalty for nearby
units, +10% ranged damage upgraded — a Marksman-line effect for the
whole army) · Shaman Staff (+6→9 Spellpower — the biggest raw caster
stat documented) · Caduceus (+4 mana per Heroic Strike use).

**Torso:** Vortex Robe (restores a large share of mana per spell cast
— reported as 50% of cost, with an enemy-cast variant at 40→60%; the
premier sustain item for casters either way).

**Waist:** Skull of Milos (+1,000 gold *per day*, Legendary — the
earlier found, the more it prints) · Spells in Bottles (all Tier-4
spells).

**Feet:** Warlord Boots (+2 Initiative to all friendly units — turn
order as an item) · Legion's Leg (**+50% unit growth in all cities**
— the single biggest economy artifact documented; note the overlap
with the Herald's Progenitor subclass for a truly absurd Hive stack).

**Back/misc:** Light-and-Shade Cloak (ranged attacks usable at close
range — deletes the shooter's one weakness) · Fallen Angel Wings
(flight over obstacles — Shadowflight, permanently) · Tranquil Glass
(fully restores the hero's mana every day) · Quivering Quiver
(ranged-army staple) · mana-pool misc items (+30 combined from the
two standard picks).

**Relics:** the **Holy Sigil of Roph** is the documented archetype —
all unlearned subskills of every *faction* skill (Righteousness,
Necromancy, Murmuring, Triumvirate's Strength, Abyssal Communion,
Summon Swarm). There is no "best relic" in general; each maps to a
skill package, so the right one is whichever completes *your* build.

## Build packages (original analysis)

- **The Caster Engine:** Shaman Staff + Vortex Robe + Tranquil Glass
  + Fine Wand (+1 level to all spells) — Spellpower, sustain, and a
  free spell-level; every magic chapter's numbers scale up a bracket.
- **The Ranged Army Kit:** Shroomwood Bow + Light-and-Shade Cloak +
  Quivering Quiver + Warlord Boots — removes every structural
  weakness this book's shooter-core advice carries.
- **The Economy Snowball:** Skull of Milos + Legion's Leg — gold and
  growth; strongest in the earliest weeks you can assemble it.
- **The Morale Stack:** Knight's Honor set + Ukh Signet + Resolve
  subskill + Encouragement law — the combat appendix's 20%-per-point
  ceiling, itemized.
