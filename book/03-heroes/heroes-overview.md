# Heroes — who leads matters as much as what follows

## How heroes work (verified)

Every hero is defined by four things:

1. **Type** — Might or Magic, which decides the exclusive skill
   available (Combat for Might, Thaumaturgy for Magic) and the stat
   growth lean.
2. **Class** — the faction pairing of one Might and one Magic class:
   Knight/Cleric (Temple), Death Knight/Necromancer (Necropolis),
   Warden/Druid (Grove), Enforcer/Herald (Hive), Schism and Dungeon's
   pairs (Riftspeaker; Warlock). Class determines the faction skill
   and the subclass paths (five specific Expert skills each).
3. **Specialty** — the hero's permanent, unique bonus. This is the
   real differentiator, and the master table reveals it comes in
   **six recurring archetypes** (below).
4. **Starting skills & spells** — every hero begins with their
   faction skill plus one other skill (or, for a few, *Advanced*
   faction skill alone), and sometimes a starting spell.

## The six specialty archetypes (verified pattern)

Reading the full master table, every hero specialty in the game falls
into one of six molds — knowing them lets you evaluate any hero at a
glance:

**1. Masterful-spell specialists.** Start with the unique fifth-tier
version of one spell, usually with +1 effective Spellpower per 2 hero
levels for that spell (variants exist: Artorius Veritas gets mana-cost
reduction instead; Sister Deira and Vatawna scale per 3 levels).
Documented in full in the magic chapters.

**2. School-masters** — the archetype this pass discovered. They get
**+1 level to ALL spells of one school** (+1 more per 10 hero
levels), casting that school doesn't lock out its siblings that
round, **and the enemy hero cannot cast that school at all.**
Verified: **Lia the Untethered One** (Temple — Daylight), **Mag**
(Necropolis — Arcane), **Shadespinner Oona** (Necropolis —
Nightshade). A school-master is effectively playing every spell in
their school one level up *while deleting that school from the
enemy's book* — arguably the strongest specialty archetype in the
game for caster mains.

**3. Creature specialists.** One unit line gets +1–2 town growth,
and under the hero's command: +1 Speed, +1 Initiative, +20% HP, and
+1 Attack/Defense per 3 hero levels — while *enemy* units of the same
type lose the same amount. Verified examples: John Johnson
(Swordsmen), Kestrel (Crossbowmen), Keandra (Cavalry), Zenith
(Lightweavers), Onkos (Skeletons, +2 growth), Kel'Ghul (Dread
Knights), Natalida (Undead Pets), Adahn (Wights), Ethric (Liches),
Guildmaster Klastor (Graverobbers), Gingertail (Fauns), Leira
(Hornets).

**4. Economy specialists.** Resource income plus doubled map
pickups: Clarissa & Milossa the Golden (+500 gold/day +100/3 lvls,
+25% map gold), Heretic Avis (Gems), Zam (Mercury), Mreowa
(Crystals) — note each rare-resource specialist maps to their
faction's T7 gate (Temple/Gems, Necropolis/Mercury, Grove/Crystals).

**5. Army-wide stat auras.** Bulwark (-10% damage taken from basic
attacks, scaling), Gorel Spearhead (+10% damage dealt, scaling), Lord
Edgar (troops gain 10% of *his* Attack/Defense), Aeos the Exalted
(+1 Morale and the strongest morale-trigger amplifier in the game),
Octavia (the Luck mirror of Aeos).

**6. Map/meta specialists.** Ister (+5% movement per 8 levels, road
bonus), Leon Sticky-Fingers & Eith (no terrain penalties, +sight),
Pip (+1 attribute point per 2 levels, +XP — the compounding pick),
Merry Elias (+1 use of every map spell daily, +max mana),
King-of-Kings (keeps army when fleeing neutrals, +Persuasion),
Tarius & Funerella (+% Necromancy Power).

## Choosing a main hero (original analysis)

1. **School-masters > single Masterful spells for caster mains** —
   +1 level to twenty spells (plus denying the enemy the school)
   compounds harder than one perfect spell. Masterful specialists
   shine as *seconds* built around their one trick, or when the trick
   is game-warping (Rewind Life, Berserk, Fireball).
2. **Creature specialists want commitment** — the +1 Atk/Def per 3
   levels *and* the enemy-debuff mirror only pay if that unit line is
   genuinely your core. Zenith and Onkos qualify; a specialist for a
   line you'll bench does not.
3. **Economy heroes are second heroes** — +1 Gem/day is a great
   background job and a terrible main character.
4. **Match the archetype to the faction's clock** — tempo factions
   (Hive, Dungeon) want specialties online at level 1 (Mila, The Eye
   Collective, Zenith); inevitability factions (Necropolis) want
   compounding ones (Lord Rufus, Pip-style XP scaling, Tarius).
