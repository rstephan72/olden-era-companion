# Decision Trees — the book, compressed into flowcharts

Every tree here is a compression of chapters you've already read (or
can skip to). They're built for the moment of decision, not for
study — the prose after each one tells you which chapter to open when
the compressed answer isn't enough. All diagrams are original to this
book.

## Tree 1: Which faction should I play?

![Faction picker decision tree](assets/diagrams/faction-picker-tree.svg)

The tree's first question — *what do you want wins to feel like?* —
is doing real work: the six factions genuinely divide into three
temperaments. **Safe & steady** (Temple's buffed line, Grove's
penalty-free shooters), **inevitable** (Necropolis's harvest, Schism's
streaks), **fast & sharp** (Dungeon's precision, Hive's swarm). The
two orange boxes are honest warnings, not insults: Schism and Hive
carry the book's 2/5 beginner-friendliness scores, and both chapters
explain exactly why. The bottom check is the Matchup Grid's clock
rule — tempo picks are on a timer against the inevitability picks.
Full reasoning: the six faction chapters' verdicts.

## Tree 2: Should I take this fight?

![Fight selection decision tree](assets/diagrams/fight-selection-tree.svg)

The most expensive mistakes in a campaign are fights you shouldn't
have taken — and fights you shouldn't have *skipped*. The tree
encodes the Foundations chapter's Week 1 doctrine: intel first (Read
Minds costs 7 mana; a wrong guess costs stacks), zero-loss fights are
always taken (XP compounds), and losses are only acceptable when they
buy compounding assets — a mine, an artifact, a dwelling — *and* the
army regrows this week. The "wait one growth week" box is the most
under-used button in the genre: the same fight is cheaper next
Monday. Necropolis players: your tree tilts hard toward "take it" —
fights are your economy (faction chapter).

## Tree 3: What should my hero cast this round?

![Round-by-round casting decision tree](assets/diagrams/round-decision-tree.svg)

Eighty-three spells reduce to five questions, in strict priority
order: **protect** (a fight-losing hit outranks everything —
Carapace for one big hit, Taunt/Radiant Armour for many), **amplify**
(if the army attacks a target this round, Vulnerability comes
*first* — the sequencing lesson the Nightshade chapter repeats),
**control** (one problem stack → remove it from the game, after
checking the immunity lists), **area** (2+ adjacent stacks → the AoE
suite; Black Hole manufactures its own clusters), and only then
**default single-target value**. "Bank mana + Heroic Strike" is a
legitimate answer — the Foundations chapter's free-spell framing.

## How to read any tree in this book

1. Trees encode *priority order*, not exhaustive options — when two
   boxes both apply, the higher one wins.
2. Every leaf has a chapter behind it; the tree is the index, the
   chapter is the argument.
3. Trees are the most patch-fragile content in the book: they
   compress verdicts, and verdicts move. Check the chapter's
   `patch_verified` date before trusting a leaf in a tournament.
