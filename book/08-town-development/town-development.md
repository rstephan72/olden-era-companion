# Town Development — the other half of every turn

Armies win battles; towns win campaigns. This chapter covers the
verified building categories, the Laws system, and the build-order
principles the faction chapters apply — plus the two faction-specific
Week 1 plans this book has verified sourcing for.

## What a town builds (verified categories)

- **Creature dwellings** — one per tier, each unlocking that unit's
  weekly growth (the verified rates run from Skeletons' 30/week to
  T7s' 1–2/week). Upgraded dwellings unlock the dual upgrade paths
  documented in every faction's stat tables. External map dwellings
  supplement town growth (and are what Reinforcements/Relocation
  interact with remotely).
- **Mage Guild** — unlocks spells for visiting heroes, and each guild
  built is worth **6 points** toward your Neutral-spell auto-leveling
  (the single biggest points item in that system; see the Neutral
  Magic chapter). Ordinary buildings are worth 2 points each — every
  construction day quietly levels your logistics suite.
- **Marketplace** — resource conversion at a loss; the verified
  framing from the Schism sourcing is "conversion fallback" for
  rare-resource bottlenecks, not a plan.
- **Artifact merchant** — buyable artifacts (verified in the
  Artifacts chapter's acquisition list).
- **Fortifications** — walls/towers/gate, the siege math from the
  Foundations chapter; the defender's half of "campaigns are decided
  at gates."
- **Faction-unique structures** — e.g. Schism's Eerie Summoning Rite
  (the T7 Envoy building, verified on the Envoy's page).

## The Laws system (verified)

Each faction has **Laws** — a parallel investment track upgraded with
**seals**, separate from gold construction. Verified mechanics from
the official Laws pages: each Law has **3 levels**, each level
improving only the numeric values of the same effect; in-game
tooltips show your current benefit and the post-upgrade cumulative
one. Laws are faction-flavored passives that reach outside the town —
the one verified by name in this book is **Encouragement** (+2%
morale-trigger chance per point), a required piece of the combat
appendix's 20%-per-morale-point ceiling; and the Abyssal Envoy's page
notes it is "specifically affected by" certain Schism Laws — Laws
tune units, not just economy. Full per-faction Law tables: tracked as
an open gap (six pages, same merged-tooltip format).

## Build-order principles (original analysis, anchored to verified plans)

**The two verified Week 1 plans in this book:**

*Dungeon (from the pilot chapter):* Day 1 Amphitheater → Days 2–3
Byzantine Palace (T2/T3 access) → Day 4 Labyrinth → Days 5–7 flex to
map circumstances. The shape: **income first, then the cheapest
real-army tiers, then commit.**

*Schism (verified third-party build order):* early army from
T1–T2 dwellings with Cultist ranged damage → Days 3–4 split Mage
Guild + Marketplace → **Arbitrators (T6) dwelling accessible by the
start of Week 2** → explicitly do *not* rush the T7 building
("puts too much pressure on the economy"), and when forced to choose
between a building and recruiting, **recruit**.

**The principles those two plans share — apply to any faction:**

1. **Never skip a build day.** Towns build one structure per day;
   a skipped day is unrecoverable. Even a "wrong" cheap building
   beats nothing (and pays 2 Neutral points).
2. **Dwellings before upgrades, breadth before height.** Weekly
   growth starts accruing the day the dwelling exists — a dwelling
   built Day 2 instead of Day 5 is permanent extra army. Dual
   upgrades can wait; missed growth can't.
3. **Target a named Week 2 milestone.** Every strong verified plan
   has one (Dungeon's Labyrinth line, Schism's Arbitrators, the
   Necropolis sourcing's "Dread Knight building by early Week 2,"
   Temple's Lightweavers — or Zenith, who *is* the milestone,
   starting with them). Pick the faction chapter's flagged core
   unit and build the shortest path to it.
4. **The T7 building is a trap on schedule.** Every faction's T7 is
   rare-resource gated (Foundations chapter); the building without
   the Gems/Mercury/Crystal to recruit from it is dead gold. Rush it
   only when the resource pipeline exists — which is what the
   economy-specialist second heroes are for.
5. **Recruit > build when the map is hot.** Buildings compound but
   armies act; the verified Schism guidance generalizes — an
   unrecruited growth pool is the one town resource that *doesn't*
   carry forward safely (enemy heroes exist).
6. **Growth multipliers outrank everything late.** The verified
   stack: Legion's Leg artifact (+50% growth, all cities) and the
   Herald's Progenitor subclass (+200%) turn each additional town
   into multiples — which is why the mid-game priority shifts from
   building your first town tall to taking your second and third.

## Multi-town play (original analysis)

Once you hold two-plus towns, the verified logistics suite becomes
the actual build order: Town Portal (garrison anywhere), Relocation
(collect growth remotely), Assemble!/Gate of Light (feed the front).
The town-development decision stops being "what do I build" and
becomes "which town builds army, which builds economy" — army towns
want dwellings and fortifications; economy towns want the Mage Guild
(points + spells), Marketplace, and merchant. Your capital should
usually be the army town: it's the one you'll defend.
