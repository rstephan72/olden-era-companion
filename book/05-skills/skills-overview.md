# Hero Skills — the complete taxonomy

## How the system works (verified)

On level-up a hero gains a random stat point and a choice of **three
options** — new skills or upgrades to learned ones. Every skill has
three ranks: **Basic → Advanced → Expert.**

The depth is in the forks: reaching **Advanced offers a choice of 3
subskills** (pick one); reaching **Expert offers a choice of 2 more,
stronger subskills** (pick one). You are permanently specializing at
every rank-up — with one documented exception: the **Holy Sigil of
Roph** (Epic Relic) grants *all unlearned subskills* of every faction
skill while worn.

Subskills carry **cross-skill synergies** — explicit "doubled if the
hero knows X" clauses that reward planned builds over random rolls
(examples below).

## The full skill list (verified)

**Might Skills:** Offense · Defense · Leadership · Luck · Resistance ·
Tactics · Battlecraft · Siegecraft · Recruitment · **Combat**
(Might-hero exclusive)

**Magic Skills:** Battle Magic · Sorcery · Summon Avatar · Wisdom ·
Daylight Magic · Nightshade Magic · Arcane Magic · Primal Magic ·
**Thaumaturgy** (Magic-hero exclusive)

**General Skills:** Diplomacy · Logistics · Scouting · Insight ·
Economy

**Faction Skills** (auto-known by that faction's heroes, unavailable
elsewhere): Righteousness (Temple) · Necromancy (Necropolis) ·
Murmuring (Grove) · Summon Swarm (Hive) · Abyssal Communion (Schism) ·
Triumvirate's Strength (Dungeon) — each fully documented in its
faction chapter; the four magic-school skills are covered by their
spell chapters (each school skill gates Tier 3–5 spells at
Basic/Advanced/Expert respectively); Summon Avatar is covered in the
Neutral Magic chapter.

## The Offense tree — a complete worked example (verified)

| Rank | Effect / subskill options |
|---|---|
| Basic | Basic attacks +10% damage |
| Advanced | +15% damage, then choose one: **Archery** (Ranged/Long Reach +15%) · **Battle March** (+1 Focus Point per attack — *doubled if hero knows Luck*) · **Battle Frenzy** (+2 Attack per point of Morale) |
| Expert | +20% damage, then choose one: **Shadow Blades** (+1 flat damage — the frequent-attacker's pick, per the Heavenly Blades lesson) · **Reality Wardens** (+50% damage vs. summoned creatures — *doubled if hero knows Nightshade Magic*) · **Firmness** (−15% enemy Defense) |

Reading the design from this tree: Advanced picks are playstyle forks
(ranged vs. Focus economy vs. morale builds), Expert picks are meta
answers — Reality Wardens at an effective +100% vs. summons is the
skill-tree half of the anti-Schism/anti-summon toolkit this book keeps
flagging.

## The Defense tree (verified fragments)

Basic −10% from basic attacks → Advanced −15%, with documented
subskill options including −20% vs. Ranged/Long Reach, **+1 Focus
Point when taking damage** (*doubled if hero knows Recruitment* — the
tank-battery build), and +2 Defense per point of Luck → Expert −20%,
with options including −1 flat enemy damage and −10% Magic Damage
(*doubled if hero knows Diplomacy*).

## Other verified subskill highlights

- **Leadership/Luck:** flat hero Morale (+1/+2 documented) and the
  capstones **Resolve** (+1% extra-turn chance per Morale point,
  *doubled by Battlecraft*) and **Beastly Confidence** (+1% Lucky
  Strike per Luck point, *doubled by Primal Magic*) — the engine of
  the 20%-per-morale-point stack documented in the combat appendix.
- **Tactics:** widens your deployment zone (3 → 4 → 5 lines) and
  *shrinks the enemy's* if they also know Tactics — the only directly
  adversarial skill; also documented: +2 Attack per adjacent enemy
  (*doubled by Offense*) and a once-per-round +2 Initiative trigger
  when an enemy waits.
- **Resistance:** includes a granted ability dealing heavy Magic
  Damage to a target *summoned* creature, and an effect stripping 1
  level from all the enemy's Magic School spells — the anti-caster
  tree.
- **Sorcery-adjacent:** a documented "+1 Focus Charge whenever the
  enemy casts" trigger — casters feeding your economy.

## Build guidance (original analysis)

1. **Plan around the doublers.** The synergy clauses are the skill
   system's real currency: Battle March + Luck, Resolve + Battlecraft,
   Reality Wardens + Nightshade, Beastly Confidence + Primal. A build
   that lines up two doublers outperforms a higher-level hero who
   rolled randomly.
2. **Might heroes:** Offense/Defense core, Tactics into the
   adversarial clause vs. human-style opponents, Leadership for
   Living/Hive armies — and *never* morale skills for Undead or
   Construct armies (caps locked at 0; see the combat appendix).
3. **Magic heroes:** your school skill is mandatory (it gates Tier 3–5
   spells), Wisdom and Sorcery support it, Summon Avatar is the best
   free stats in the game (Neutral chapter, 4/5).
4. **Everyone:** the subclass system (five specific Expert skills →
   subclass, per hero-class pages like the Herald's Progenitor)
   makes Expert-rank choices campaign-length commitments — check your
   target subclass's prerequisites *before* spending Expert picks.
