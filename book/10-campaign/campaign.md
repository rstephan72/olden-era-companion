# Campaign Play — the long game

This book rates everything ⚔️ for campaign because campaign is where
the game's systems compound longest. This chapter collects the
campaign-specific doctrine the other chapters built, plus what's
verified about the setting. What it deliberately doesn't contain:
mission-by-mission walkthroughs — the Early Access campaign is still
growing, no reliable mission sourcing meets this book's standard yet,
and inventing walkthroughs is exactly what the audits exist to
prevent. Tracked as the category's open gap.

## The world you're fighting over (verified lore)

The setting is **Jadame**, and the six factions' verified blurbs
sketch the war: the Church of the Sun's High Inquisition (Temple) in
renewed conflict with its old enemy **Shadowspire**, home of the
Necromancers' Guild (Necropolis); the **Triumvirate of Alvar** —
"formerly underground, currently backstage rulers of Jadame"
(Dungeon); a splinter cult of Alvari elves in the Frozen Vast who
tore open a Rift after **Lyssara's** failed expedition (Schism — and
she "insists they are under her control," per the Envoy's own page,
unconvincingly); **Beelzebub's Order of the Fly** under the Dragonfly
King, "bent on conquering Jadame" (Hive); and the Murmurwoods, whose
governing body is officially *Unknown* (Grove). Two verified
character threads worth watching for: the twinned sisters **Deira**
(Dungeon) and **Keiri** (Schism), and campaign-obtainable artifacts —
the **Fine Wand** (+1 level to all spells) is confirmed as a campaign
mission reward.

## The five doctrines (the book's campaign advice, consolidated)

**1. Zero-loss is the win condition inside the win condition.**
Campaigns are sequences; losses in mission N are weakness in mission
N+1. The verified toolkit, in escalating order: fight selection
(Tree 2), range + control (Web/Twilight), summons as ammunition
(Starchild, Larvae, Communion units — free deaths), healing
(Healing Water at 6 mana), and the crown — **Rewind Life**, the
book's only 5/5 campaign spell, because it makes losses *reversible*.

**2. Compounding picks beat peak picks.** A campaign hero levels for
dozens of hours: Pip's +1 attribute per 2 levels, school-masters'
per-10-level scaling, specialists' per-2-level spell bonuses, and
economy artifacts found early (Skull of Milos) all out-earn flashier
immediate power. The heroes chapter's archetype guidance doubles in
weight here.

**3. The weekly rhythm is the real difficulty setting.** Never skip
a build day, never leave growth unrecruited before a hot border,
never end a week without spending the logistics suite (Town Portal /
Relocation are rated 5/5 and 4/5 *for this mode*). Campaign
difficulty is mostly self-inflicted tempo loss.

**4. Learn the counter-tech before you need it.** The matchup grid's
recurring swing cards — Fatal Decay (4 mana, switches off entire
sustain factions), anti-summon effects (Heavenly Blades L4,
Anti-Magic), Wean against buff-stackers — are cheap to carry and
mission-deciding when the campaign throws the matching enemy at you.
Campaign enemies telegraph their faction; read the grid's row before
the mission, not during.

**5. Bank flexibility for the unknown.** Early Access campaigns
change under your feet (Schism's post-Patch-8 re-tuning is the
verified example). Favor heroes and builds this book rates for
breadth — school-masters, the four-currency conversion framing from
Foundations — over single-trick builds a patch can delete.

## Difficulty & mode notes (original analysis, flagged as such)

Where a campaign offers hero carryover between missions, doctrine 2
becomes doctrine zero — every level and artifact is permanent
infrastructure, and slow-rolling a won mission to over-level is
legitimate play. Where it doesn't, doctrine 3 dominates: fresh starts
reward the players with the cleanest Week 1s. The book's faction
⚔️ scores already assume campaign context; for a first campaign,
the introduction's reading order stands — Temple, then branch out.
