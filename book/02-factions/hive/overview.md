# Hive — The Order of the Fly

> ⭐ Power: 3/5 · 🟢 Beginner Friendly: 2/5 · ⚔️ Campaign: 3/5 ·
> 💰 Economy: 3/5 · 🧠 Skill Ceiling: 5/5 ·
> 📈 Early 4/5 · Mid 3/5 · Late 3/5

**In one sentence:** The swarm-aggression faction — the Dragonfly
King's insect legions overwhelm through speed, numbers, and
ability-driven units, rewarding players who attack constantly and
punishing everyone who hesitates.

**Identity (per the official faction page):** Beelzebub's Order of the
Fly, commanded by the Dragonfly King, "bent on conquering Jadame" and
"driven by swift and aggressive tactics." Community tier assessments
consistently place Hive among the factions "requiring much higher
mechanical skill and specific conditions to reach full potential" —
this is the expert's aggression faction, not the beginner's.

***

## Faction skill: Summon Swarm

The Hive-exclusive skill (all Hive heroes start with some rank of it —
e.g. Mila begins with Basic Summon Swarm per her official page).

**What it does (official mechanics, verified):** once per round, the
hero **lays a clutch of eggs on the battlefield; at the start of the
next round they hatch into a stack of Fire Larvae** whose total HP
equals a percentage of the combined HP of all Hive Spawns in the
hero's army — **8% at Basic, 12% at Advanced, 16% at Expert** — with
ability strength scaling with hero level. The eggs are attackable
(destroyed after two hits), adding a mind-game: enemies must waste
attacks on eggs or accept the hatch.

**The math that matters:** the summon scales with *your army's total
HP*, so it grows all game — a late-game army with 3,000 combined Hive
HP hatches a 480-HP larva stack at Expert, *every round, forever*.
Free bodies as a percentage of your bodies: the swarm that spawns
swarms. This also confirms the roster's structural tell — Fire Larva
sits outside the upgrade system entirely because it's the skill's
output, not a recruited unit.

**Class note — the Herald (Hive's Magic class):** officially
documented subclasses are **Progenitor** (+200% creature growth in
all your cities — on the swarm faction, an obscene multiplier) and
**Lord of Chaos** (Heroic Strike deals +2 damage per hero attribute
point). Progenitor is the campaign-defining one: it's the biggest
growth multiplier printed anywhere in the game.

## Abilities — the passive and Focus layer

How this works (verified from first-party documentation): every unit
carries up to two kinds of special abilities. **Passives are always
on** — auras, triggers, movement types, attack-type clauses. **Actives
cost Focus Points**, which units accumulate by attacking and being
attacked; bank enough and the ability fires. This layer is where
Olden Era's combat depth actually lives — two stacks with identical
stat lines can play completely differently — and it's what the Focus
economy tools in this book (Murmuring, Energize, Battle March, Fancy
Mask, Silence) exist to accelerate or deny.

**Verified Hive abilities:**

| Unit | Ability | Type | Effect |
|---|---|---|---|
| Parasite line | Long Reach | Passive | Hits the hex behind an adjacent hex — no counterattack, no movement |
| Pyroboros | Ranged, no melee penalty | Passive | The swarm's surprise artillery keeps full damage in contact |
| Hive Huntress | No Counterattack | Passive | 70-damage strikes that are never punished |
| Chanter | Support flyer profile | Passive | Speed 11 / Initiative 4 — a late-round, long-range ability carrier |
| Fire Larva | Expendable | — | The Summon Swarm output; exists to absorb hits and Focus-farm |

The faction keyword is *Creature Abilities*, and the roster's named
actives (Scorpion, Reaver, Waurms, Queen lines) are the audit's
remaining per-unit fetch. Two verified mechanics already define the
Focus game here:

**Focus priorities:** Hive both *generates* and *feeds on* Focus
faster than anyone — Long Reach T1s trading safely builds charges,
and every egg the enemy is forced to kill (two hits each) hands your
survivors more. The verified loop: Summon Swarm eggs → enemy wastes
attacks → your real stacks bank Focus → actives fire first. Pair with
the Arcane Energize package (the faction chapter's standing
recommendation) and round one opens with abilities already loaded.
Fear enemy **Silence** above any damage spell — it switches this
entire layer off.

## Recommended magic schools (cross-referenced)

**Primary: Daylight — one spell above all: Haste, because Hive owns
its Masterful version.** **Mila (Herald) starts with Masterful Haste**
— army-wide speed, +1 effective Spellpower per 2 levels — on the
faction whose official identity is "swift and aggressive tactics."
It's the single cleanest faction/spell fit in the game: every point of
Speed converts directly into earlier contact, and earlier contact is
the whole Hive thesis. (Mila also starts with Basic Daylight Magic,
per her official page — the faction's magic class is genuinely built
for this school.)

**Primary co-pick: Arcane's Focus package.** The faction keyword
*Creature Abilities* + ability-driven units = Energize and Energy
Explosion (see the Arcane chapter) are worth more here than to any
other faction: front-loading Focus charges means your units' actives
fire on round one, matching the tempo plan.

**Situational:** Nightshade's Silence is the *mirror-match terror* —
an enemy silencing your ability-dependent army removes most of what
makes it dangerous; know the threat even if you never cast it.

## Hero picks

- **Mila (the flagship):** Masterful Haste on the speed faction. The
  obvious pick is also the correct one. Her lore (a Church deserter
  answered by the Dragonfly King) even makes her the thematic bridge
  from Temple's buff school to Hive's aggression.
- **Leira (the creature route):** Hornets specialist — the pick when
  you'd rather scale the core stack than the tempo.
- **Any Herald → Progenitor:** the +200% growth subclass is a
  campaign plan by itself: chase its five prerequisite skills and the
  swarm becomes a flood.
- **Niev:** magic-damage specialist (per official patch notes) for a
  more caster-forward Hive.

## Three builds

**1. The First Wave (tempo):** Mila + Masterful Haste + Hornet core +
Fire Larva screens. Contact on turn one, everywhere, always. The
faction played as designed.

**2. Progenitor's Flood (campaign):** any Herald raced to the
Progenitor subclass; +200% growth turns the numbers advantage into
something no economy matches. Slower to come online; unanswerable
after.

**3. Focus Storm (advanced):** Energize/Energy Explosion package +
ability-heavy units (Scorpions/Reavers) — every fight opens with a
full ability salvo. The highest-skill expression of the roster.

## Matchups (vs. completed chapters)

Against **Dungeon:** the tempo mirror — Aureate Dancer initiative vs.
Masterful Haste; whoever lands the first real volley usually wins the
war. Against **Temple:** their wall (Sun's Aegis/Radiant Armour)
exists to blunt exactly your alpha strike; Wean their buffs or go
around. Against **Necropolis:** win by week 4 or watch the snowball
bury the swarm; also note their undead are Sleep-immune just like your
Spawns — the control spells fizzle in both directions. Against
**Grove:** race their casters — every round you give Faleor and Halon
is a stack you lose. Full matchup grid: STUB.

## Common mistakes
- Playing Hive patiently. The faction's mid/late curve (3/5) exists
  precisely because its early tempo (4/5) is the win condition —
  hesitation converts your best phase into your worst.
- Spending Fire Larvae like real units instead of screens — they're
  the faction's renewable resource; their job is dying usefully.
- Ignoring Focus economy on an ability-keyword faction.
- Underestimating enemy Silence in caster matchups.

## Verdict: ★★★☆☆ (3/5)
The highest skill ceiling in the game attached to a genuinely scary
tempo plan — and the honest rating reflects that most players won't
pilot it to its potential. In expert hands with Mila's Masterful Haste
or a completed Progenitor arc, it plays a full tier above this score;
on autopilot, a full tier below.
