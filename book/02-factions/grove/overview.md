# Grove — The Murmurwoods

> ⭐ Power: 4/5 · 🟢 Beginner Friendly: 4/5 · ⚔️ Campaign: 4/5 ·
> 💰 Economy: 3/5 · 🧠 Skill Ceiling: 3/5 ·
> 📈 Early 3/5 · Mid 4/5 · Late 5/5

**In one sentence:** The magic faction — elemental spirits that are
each "a unique powerhouse," the safest ranged roster in the game for
newer players, and the deepest bench of *elemental-damage* hero
specialists anywhere, at the price of the most expensive units.

**Identity (per the official faction page):** the Murmurwoods'
governing body is officially *Unknown*; elementals & magical
creatures; keywords *Strong Spellcasters* and *Creature Abilities*.
Community assessments consistently call it the safest,
most beginner-friendly ranged roster — with "strong and expensive"
as the official description of its rangers and druids, and that cost
is the faction's real constraint.

***

## Faction skill: Murmuring

Known by default by all Wardens and Druids (Grove's two hero classes),
unavailable elsewhere.

**What it does (official mechanics, verified):** the hero **starts
every battle with Focus Charges already banked** — 1 at Basic, 2 at
Advanced, 3 at Expert. Focus is the resource creatures spend on their
active abilities, so this skill means Grove's ability-rich roster (and
its hero) opens fights with its best tricks already loaded instead of
grinding attacks to earn them.

**Documented subskills:** Mycelium Grounds (+10% dealt / -10% taken on
Autumn terrain), Life Essence (+1 Focus Charge cap), Child of the
Woods (+20 max mana); at Expert: Just One More Time (every Focus spend
cuts all the hero's spell cooldowns by 2 rounds), Burst of Energy
(Focus spends add +15% per hero level to Heroic Strike damage), and
Strong Connection (each Focus spend gives the hero +2 to all
attributes until end of round).

**Why it fits:** the Expert tree turns Focus spending into a
hero-stat engine — and note the direct synergy with Arcane's
Energize/Energy Explosion package (see that chapter): Grove is the one
faction whose faction skill *feeds* the Focus-economy combo natively.

## Abilities — the passive and Focus layer

How this works (verified from first-party documentation): every unit
carries up to two kinds of special abilities. **Passives are always
on** — auras, triggers, movement types, attack-type clauses. **Actives
cost Focus Points**, which units accumulate by attacking and being
attacked; bank enough and the ability fires. This layer is where
Olden Era's combat depth actually lives — two stacks with identical
stat lines can play completely differently — and it's what the Focus
economy tools in this book (Murmuring, Energize, Battle March, Fancy
Mask, Silence) exist to accelerate or deny.

**Verified Grove abilities:** this roster's special layer is unusually
*passive*-weighted — its clauses are printed on the attack types
rather than banked in Focus bars:

| Unit | Ability | Type | Effect |
|---|---|---|---|
| Faun | Ranged, no melee penalty | Passive | Full-strength shots even when engaged |
| Faun Archer | No penalties at all | Passive | Ignores distance falloff *and* melee penalty |
| Dawn Hoplet | No Counterattack | Passive | Its melee strikes are never punished |
| Murmurmancer | Fixed 18–18 damage | Passive | Zero-variance volleys |
| Qilin line | Native Luck 3 | Passive | Constant Lucky Strike pressure (+18% base chance) |
| Herbomancer line | Long Reach | Passive | Attacks over the front rank without provoking |

The named *active* abilities of the Iriyad/Herbomancer/Phoenix lines
(the wiki's per-unit pages carry them) are the category's remaining
fetch — tracked in the audit. What's already verified reshapes play:

**Focus priorities:** Grove is the faction where the *hero* is the
Focus engine — Murmuring hands you 1–3 charges at battle start before
any unit swings. Until the per-unit actives are documented, the
verified play is spending that head start through the Expert
Murmuring subskills (cooldown refunds, Heroic Strike riders, +2 all
attributes per spend) — the hero converts the faction's Focus surplus
even when units bank theirs.

## Recommended magic schools (cross-referenced)

**Primary: Primal — Grove owns this school at the hero level.** Both
of Primal's confirmed Masterful specialists are Grove heroes:
**Faleor (Masterful Fireball** — bigger area; the pick community
guides call Grove's best hero outright**)** and **Halon (Masterful
Chain Lightning** — half damage loss per jump, pushing chain totals
toward ~1,800). The logic is airtight for the faction's problem
profile: expensive units mean every fight should be won *before
contact*, and turn-one multi-stack AoE is exactly that. Read those two
entries in the Primal chapter first.

**Primary co-pick: Arcane.** **Vatawna's Masterful Mirror Copy**
(clones *enemy* stacks to fight for you) is Grove's third confirmed
Masterful hero — and Rewind Life is even more valuable here than
elsewhere because of unit prices: reviving a wiped Grove stack refunds
more gold than reviving anyone else's.

**The takeaway:** Grove has *three confirmed Masterful heroes across
two schools* — more offensive-magic specialist depth than any other
faction — plus the longest spell-specialist patch-notes cohort in the
game (Old Pilgrim, Aeliniel, Glacia, Vim, Echolily, Sullie alongside
the three above). If you want to play the game as a wizard with an
army attached rather than the reverse, this is the faction.

## Hero picks

- **Faleor (the default):** Masterful Fireball from level 1 solves the
  fragile-expensive-early-game problem by killing threats before they
  arrive. Best first pick.
- **Halon (the late-game ceiling):** Masterful Chain Lightning; the
  single highest sustained-nuke output in the game once leveled.
- **Vatawna (the trickster):** Masterful Mirror Copy targeting enemy
  stacks — steals the enemy's best unit as a battle plan.
- **Aunt Daliar (the might route):** Warden (Grove's Might class),
  Insight specialist — the pick for players running Grove's army as
  the main act rather than the supporting cast.
- **Alluring Sh'a:** diplomacy specialist — converting neutral armies
  to your side sidesteps the faction's gold-cost problem entirely, a
  quietly perfect thematic fit.

## Three builds

**1. The Patient Forest (beginner):** Faun Archers + ranged core
behind Hoplet screens; Faleor clearing approaches with Fireball. The
"safest ranged roster" reputation, played straight.

**2. Stormcaller (mid/late):** Halon + maximum Spellpower investment;
army exists to hold the line while Chain Lightning does the actual
killing. Grove as a delivery system for one spell.

**3. The Mirrorwood (advanced):** Vatawna stealing enemy elites +
Alluring Sh'a's diplomacy feeding converted neutrals into the army —
Grove's gold problem solved by never paying for units at all.

## Matchups (vs. completed chapters)

Against **Dungeon:** a shooters-vs-shooters race where Dungeon's
Initiative edge (Aureate Dancers) usually fires first — Grove's answer
is Faleor deleting Dancer stacks in the approach. Against **Temple:**
mirror-adjacent (both buff-and-shoot factions); Grove's AoE
specialists punish Temple's tight defensive formations, while Temple's
Wean-resistant... rather, Temple's *cleanse* suite (Inner Light)
strips Grove's setup. Against **Necropolis:** your expensive-unit
economy vs. their free one — end it early or don't. Full matchup
grid: STUB.

## Common mistakes
- Playing Grove like a might faction and bleeding gold on replacement
  units — the entire design points at spells-first play.
- Picking a generalist hero over Faleor/Halon/Vatawna and forfeiting
  the faction's defining advantage.
- Fighting attrition wars against Necropolis with the most expensive
  units in the game.
- Ignoring diplomacy tools that exist precisely to offset unit costs.

## Verdict: ★★★★☆ (4/5)
The wizard's faction, and the best expression of magic-forward play in
the game — three Masterful specialists across the two best offensive
schools, on top of a safe ranged roster. One star withheld for the
economy: everything Grove fields is priced like it's irreplaceable,
and against fast tempo or free-growth factions, sometimes it is.
