# The Matchup Grid — every faction vs. every faction

How to read this: each cell is written from the **row faction's**
perspective, grounded in the verified stat tables and skill mechanics
in the six faction chapters and the spell data in the magic chapters.
Matchups describe *structural* pressure — who must act, who profits
from time — not guaranteed outcomes; pilots beat pairings. Ratings:
**F** (favored), **E** (even), **U** (unfavored).

## The nine matchup families (row vs. column)

**Temple vs. Necropolis (E):** buffs against inevitability. Temple's
per-fight edge is real — Radiant Armour + Marksmen out-trade skeleton
volleys — but Necromancy converts every fight *anywhere on the map*
into Necropolis growth. Temple's win condition is tempo it doesn't
naturally have; its equalizer is that four of its Clerics carry
elite Masterful spells, and one 4-mana Fatal Decay (learnable
cross-school) shuts off Vampirism in the fights that matter. The clock
favors the dead; the battles favor the sun. Even.

**Temple vs. Hive (F):** the wall against the wave. Hive's entire
gameplan — Stinger Initiative 12 alpha strikes, Larva screens — runs
into the one roster built to receive it: Sun's Aegis 8-Def T1s,
Guardian Griffins, Radiant Armour L4 (-35% army-wide). Every blunted
first wave hands Righteousness stacks to the Temple hero. Hive must
hit harder than Temple can armor, and the math (Hive's fragile HP
pools vs. Temple's fixed-damage Lightweavers) says it usually can't.

**Temple vs. Schism (F):** Temple owns the two cleanest anti-Schism
tools in the game — Heavenly Blades L4 (deletes summoned units on
contact: Communion reinforcements, Starchildren, all of it) and a
roster durable enough to survive the streak-fueled opening. Break one
fight and Communion halves itself into irrelevance overnight.

**Necropolis vs. Grove (F):** free army against the most expensive
army in the game. Every Grove stack Necropolis kills costs real gold
to replace; every Necropolis stack Grove kills feeds nothing (undead
don't fear attrition) and returns via Rewind Death/Vampirism anyway.
Grove's out is Faleor/Halon ending fights before the grind starts —
possible, but the structural pressure is one-directional.

**Necropolis vs. Hive (F):** the immunity matchup. Hive's corpse
economy meets a faction whose dead *don't feed Necromancy's enemies*
— wait, reverse it: Hive Spawns DO feed Necromancy (verified
type-interaction), meaning every dead swarm unit is a future
Necropolis recruit, while Necropolis's Undead feed Hive's
consumption abilities nothing they can't get elsewhere. Add
Sleep-immunity symmetry (both sides' control fizzles) and the fight
reduces to raw attrition — the one game Necropolis never loses.

**Grove vs. Schism (F):** Grove's Focus economy (Murmuring's free
battle-start charges) comes online turn one, faster than Communion's
streak-building, and Faleor's turn-one Masterful Fireball erases the
fragile Schism early roster the moment it clusters around its
summons. Schism's Envoy-Armageddon endgame is real, but Grove's
Phoenix-Initiative endgame arrives first.

**Hive vs. Temple / Necropolis (U, U):** covered above from the other
side — the swarm's two structural nightmares: the roster that armors
through the alpha strike, and the roster that recruits the swarm's
corpses.

**Schism vs. Temple / Grove (U, U):** the anti-summon problem in two
flavors: Temple's Heavenly Blades and Grove's tempo casters. Schism's
best games are against factions without a summon answer.

**Dungeon vs. everyone (E across the board):** the verified roster
explains the row: no bad stats, no hard counters against it, and its
identity (Luck lines, no-counter Hydras, penalty-free Medusas, the
fastest T7 in the game carrying spell immunity) is *flexibility* —
Dungeon always has a game plan, and never a free win. Its matchups
are decided by the pilot's stance-skill timing and whether the
Black Dragon/Armageddon package comes online before the opponent's
own curve peaks. The one asymmetry worth naming: vs. Necropolis and
Schism, Dungeon's tempo must beat their inevitability clocks — win by
week 4–5 or the grinding factions grind.

## Three cross-cutting rules the grid keeps repeating

1. **Sustain factions beat tempo factions that hesitate** — Necropolis
   and Schism convert time into army; against them, slow play is a
   resource transfer.
2. **Anti-summon tech warps three matchups** — Heavenly Blades L4 and
   Anti-Magic swing every Schism pairing and dent Hive's Larva game;
   check the enemy faction before skipping those entries.
3. **Type-immunity fine print decides mirror-adjacent fights** —
   Sleep's exclusion list (Undead, Hive Spawn, Embodiment, Construct)
   and Necromancy's reanimation table (Living/Hive Spawn/Magic
   Creature/Dragon feed it; Undead/Embodiment/Construct don't) are
   worth memorizing; half this grid's edges come from them.
