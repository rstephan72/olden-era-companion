    Heroes — who leads matters as much as what follows¶

    How heroes work (verified)¶

    Every hero is defined by four things:

    1. Type — Might or Magic, which decides the exclusive skill available (Combat for Might, Thaumaturgy for Magic) and the stat growth lean.
    2. Class — the faction pairing of one Might and one Magic class: Knight/Cleric (Temple), Death Knight/Necromancer (Necropolis), Warden/Druid (Grove), Enforcer/Herald (Hive), Schism and Dungeon's pairs (Riftspeaker; Warlock). Class determines the faction skill and the subclass paths (five specific Expert skills each).
    3. Specialty — the hero's permanent, unique bonus. This is the real differentiator, and the master table reveals it comes in six recurring archetypes (below).
    4. Starting skills & spells — every hero begins with their faction skill plus one other skill (or, for a few, Advanced faction skill alone), and sometimes a starting spell.

    The six specialty archetypes (verified pattern)¶

    Reading the full master table, every hero specialty in the game falls into one of six molds — knowing them lets you evaluate any hero at a glance:

    1. Masterful-spell specialists. Start with the unique fifth-tier version of one spell, usually with +1 effective Spellpower per 2 hero levels for that spell (variants exist: Artorius Veritas gets mana-cost reduction instead; Sister Deira and Vatawna scale per 3 levels). Documented in full in the magic chapters.

    2. School-masters — the archetype this pass discovered. They get +1 level to ALL spells of one school (+1 more per 10 hero levels), casting that school doesn't lock out its siblings that round, and the enemy hero cannot cast that school at all. Verified: Lia the Untethered One (Temple — Daylight), Mag (Necropolis — Arcane), Shadespinner Oona (Necropolis — Nightshade). A school-master is effectively playing every spell in their school one level up while deleting that school from the enemy's book — arguably the strongest specialty archetype in the game for caster mains.

    3. Creature specialists. One unit line gets +1–2 town growth, and under the hero's command: +1 Speed, +1 Initiative, +20% HP, and +1 Attack/Defense per 3 hero levels — while enemy units of the same type lose the same amount. Verified examples: John Johnson (Swordsmen), Kestrel (Crossbowmen), Keandra (Cavalry), Zenith (Lightweavers), Onkos (Skeletons, +2 growth), Kel'Ghul (Dread Knights), Natalida (Undead Pets), Adahn (Wights), Ethric (Liches), Guildmaster Klastor (Graverobbers), Gingertail (Fauns), Leira (Hornets).

    4. Economy specialists. Resource income plus doubled map pickups: Clarissa & Milossa the Golden (+500 gold/day +100/3 lvls, +25% map gold), Heretic Avis (Gems), Zam (Mercury), Mreowa (Crystals) — note each rare-resource specialist maps to their faction's T7 gate (Temple/Gems, Necropolis/Mercury, Grove/Crystals).

    5. Army-wide stat auras. Bulwark (-10% damage taken from basic attacks, scaling), Gorel Spearhead (+10% damage dealt, scaling), Lord Edgar (troops gain 10% of his Attack/Defense), Aeos the Exalted (+1 Morale and the strongest morale-trigger amplifier in the game), Octavia (the Luck mirror of Aeos).

    6. Map/meta specialists. Ister (+5% movement per 8 levels, road bonus), Leon Sticky-Fingers & Eith (no terrain penalties, +sight), Pip (+1 attribute point per 2 levels, +XP — the compounding pick), Merry Elias (+1 use of every map spell daily, +max mana), King-of-Kings (keeps army when fleeing neutrals, +Persuasion), Tarius & Funerella (+% Necromancy Power).

    Verified rosters¶

    Temple (18 heroes: 9 Knights, 9 Clerics)¶

    Knights: Ister (movement), Leon Sticky-Fingers (pathfinding), John Johnson (Swordsmen), Kestrel (Crossbowmen), Aeos the Exalted (morale), Heretic Avis (Gems; starts Groundsight), Keandra (Cavalry), Lord Edgar (stat-share aura; the only Knight starting with Advanced Righteousness), Old Lord Mandall (Heroic Strike +damage and a stackable +10%-damage-taken mark; Combat). Clerics: Merry Elias (map-spell uses), Pip (XP/attributes), Zenith (Lightweavers), Lia the Untethered One (Daylight school-master), Julius (Masterful Healing Water), Vesper (Masterful Blessing), Anastasia the Meek (Masterful Arina's Touch), Nadir (Masterful Vulnerability — the cross-school pick), Clarissa (gold).

    Temple picks: Zenith for tempo (per the faction chapter), Lia for the pure-Daylight main (a school-master beats even the Masterful trio for breadth), Aeos as the engine of the morale-stack build in the combat appendix.

    Necropolis (18 heroes: 9 Death Knights, 9 Necromancers)¶

    Death Knights: Bulwark (defense aura), King-of-Kings (diplomacy/flee), Onkos (Skeletons +2), Kel'Ghul (Dread Knights +2), Natalida (Undead Pets +2), Artorius Veritas (Masterful Berserk — unusually, a Might hero carrying a Masterful spell, with mana-cost scaling instead of Spellpower), Marl (Masterful Web — now fully confirmed, +1 SP/2 levels), Tarius (+Necromancy Power; Advanced Necromancy start), Zam (Mercury). Necromancers: Mag (Arcane school-master — correction to earlier chapters: Mag's specialty is school mastery, not Masterful Rewind Life), Adahn (Wights), Ethric (Liches), Guildmaster Klastor (Graverobbers), Shadespinner Oona (Nightshade school-master), Laura (Masterful Despair — closing a gap the magic audit had open), Lord Rufus (Masterful Rewind Life — the corrected owner of the game's best campaign spell), Funerella (+Necromancy Power; starts with the Summon Avatar skill), Milossa the Golden (gold).

    Necropolis picks: Lord Rufus is the campaign engine the faction chapter described (the double-growth loop stands — just under his name, not Mag's). Mag remains elite for a different reason: an Arcane school-master whose enemy can't cast Arcane. Onkos supercharges the skeleton economy; Kel'Ghul accelerates the Dread Knight rush the units data endorses.

    Grove (verified through hero 7 of ~18; rest per prior sourcing)¶

    Choosing a main hero (original analysis)¶

    1. School-masters > single Masterful spells for caster mains — +1 level to twenty spells (plus denying the enemy the school) compounds harder than one perfect spell. Masterful specialists shine as seconds built around their one trick, or when the trick is game-warping (Rewind Life, Berserk, Fireball).
    2. Creature specialists want commitment — the +1 Atk/Def per 3 levels and the enemy-debuff mirror only pay if that unit line is genuinely your core. Zenith and Onkos qualify; a specialist for a line you'll bench does not.
    3. Economy heroes are second heroes — +1 Gem/day is a great background job and a terrible main character.
    4. Match the archetype to the faction's clock — tempo factions (Hive, Dungeon) want specialties online at level 1 (Mila, The Eye Collective, Zenith); inevitability factions (Necropolis) want compounding ones (Lord Rufus, Pip-style XP scaling, Tarius).
